# PET problem statement

PET studies whether classical integer families exhibit measurable, reproducible, and partially distinguishable structural signatures under recursive prime-exponent representations.

Using bounded, disjoint samples from families such as Perfect, Primorials, Hamming, and HighlyComposite numbers, PET aims to compare intra-family compactness, inter-family separation, and structural overlap through PET metrics and PET-based distances.

The goal is descriptive rather than absolute: identify where PET captures genuine structural differences, where families remain mixed, and where shape alone is insufficient for isolation.

PET does not assume universal separability and does not treat finite-sample observations as asymptotic truth.

Its purpose is to provide a reproducible lab for testing how far structural signatures can meaningfully classify integer families.
