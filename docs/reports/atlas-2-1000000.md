# PET empirical atlas report (2..1000000)

## Scope

This report summarizes a bounded PET scan for integers in the closed range `2..1000000`.

It is part of the PET empirical atlas track and is intended as a reproducible,
range-bounded structural snapshot rather than a statement of general theory.

## Reproduction

### Command

    python3 -m src.pet.cli scan 2 1000000 --jsonl docs/reports/data/scan-2-1000000.jsonl

### Summary command

    python3 tools/atlas_summary.py docs/reports/data/scan-2-1000000.jsonl > docs/reports/data/atlas-summary-2-1000000.txt

## Observations

1. The number of distinct PET shapes rises to `230` in `2..1000000`, so the structural catalog continues to expand materially at this scale.

2. The maximum node count increases to `9`, first reached at `n = 176400`.

3. The maximum branching increases to `7`, first reached at `n = 510510`, while height remains capped at `4` in this bounded scan.

4. Height `4` remains very rare (`8` cases), even across the wider range `2..1000000`.

## Interpretation and limits

This report is descriptive and range-bounded.

It does not claim asymptotic behavior, completeness of shape emergence, or permanence of the observed distributions beyond `2..1000000`.

Its purpose is to provide a reproducible empirical atlas artifact for comparison with later scan ranges.
