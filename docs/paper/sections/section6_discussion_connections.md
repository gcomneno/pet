# 6. Discussion and Connections

PET connects integer factorization with several classical areas.

## Rooted Trees

Ignoring primes, PET shapes resemble rooted trees generated by

\[
T = Z \times \mathrm{MSET}(T).
\]

However arithmetic constraints restrict allowable branching.

## Smooth Numbers

Many generator values have the form

\[
2^a3^b5^c\cdots
\]

with small primes and balanced exponents, similar to highly composite numbers.

## Geometric Interpretation

Two parameters dominate PET complexity:

- width (number of primes)
- depth (recursive exponent structure)

Empirically

\[
\text{width} \sim \log N,
\qquad
\text{depth} \sim \log\log N.
\]

This suggests a grid‑like structure underlying multiplicative trees.
